Predicting Neurodevelopmental Disability (NDD) Risk in Children

Project Overview

This project applies machine learning to investigate how a combination of individual traits, clinical history, and family environment influences the likelihood of children developing Neurodevelopmental Disabilities (NDD). Using a rich national dataset from the NSCH (National Survey of Children's Health), the study builds a predictive model to highlight key risk factors and guide actionable interventions.

Key Features: What Powers This Analysis

  • Dataset: NSCH survey data representing 50,000+ children across the U.S.
  • Goal: Predict NDD risk levels (High vs Low) using supervised learning
  • Features Analyzed:
    • Individual: Age, gender, BMI, sleep, physical activity, screen time
    • Clinical: Autism, ADHD, diabetes diagnoses
    • Environmental: Neighborhood safety, food security, special education plans, language spoken at home

Workflow

1. Data Preprocessing

  • Imputed missing values using mean strategy for numeric features
  • Encoded categorical variables as factors

2. Modeling & Evaluation

  • Trained a Random Forest classifier to model NDD risk
  • Evaluated using confusion matrix, accuracy, and ROC-AUC
  • Applied chi-square tests, correlation analysis, and box plots to extract insight

Top Predictors

  • Age at autism diagnosis (strongest signal)
  • Child’s current age
  • Neighborhood safety perception
  • Special education support status

Performance

  • Accuracy: 95%
  • AUC: 1.0, excellent separation but may signal overfitting

Group Insights: Profiles Behind the Prediction

  • High-Risk Group: Skews older, more clinical diagnoses, lower socioeconomic status
  • Low-Risk Group: Broader age spread, better family support and neighborhood conditions

Conclusion: Insight for Intervention

This project delivers a data-driven, multidimensional view of child health, showing that early diagnoses, safe environments, and targeted support plans play a pivotal role in reducing NDD risk. The findings advocate for integrated, cross-sector efforts to improve outcomes for children—combining healthcare, education, and public policy in meaningful ways.
